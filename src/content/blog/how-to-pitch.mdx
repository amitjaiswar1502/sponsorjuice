Landing brand deals starts with a clear pitch and a confident rate. Here's how to put both together — from calculating your worth to sending an email brands actually reply to.

## Know Your Numbers First

Before you email any brand, calculate your rate using metrics that matter: average views, niche, audience geography, and deliverables. Brands care about reach, not just follower count. A creator with 50,000 followers but 5,000 average views should price closer to their views, not their follower count.

Use our [free rate calculator](/calculator/tiktok) to estimate a fair range before you pitch. Adjust for your platform — [Instagram](/calculator/instagram) and [YouTube](/calculator/youtube) use different rate coefficients.

Industry research from [Influencer Marketing Hub](https://influencermarketinghub.com/influencer-rates/) confirms that engagement and views consistently outweigh raw follower counts when brands set sponsorship budgets.

## Structure Your Outreach Email

Keep it short and specific. Decision-makers skim dozens of pitches daily, so clarity wins:

1. **Subject line** — Mention the platform and your audience size (e.g., "TikTok Partnership — 50K Followers, Beauty Niche")
2. **Intro** — One sentence on who you are and your niche
3. **Metrics** — Followers, average views, audience location
4. **Rate range** — Give a range, not a single number. Ranges invite conversation.
5. **Call to action** — Ask to connect, share media kit link

Personalize at least one line for the brand. Reference a recent campaign or product you genuinely use. Generic copy-paste pitches get ignored.

## Build a Deliverables Package

Don't sell a single post — sell a package. Combine dedicated videos, Stories, bio links, and integrated mentions. Each deliverable adds value and justifies higher rates.

Example package for a mid-tier TikTok creator:

- 1 dedicated 60-second video
- 3 Story frames with swipe-up or link sticker
- 7-day bio link placement
- Usage rights for 90 days

Bundle pricing should reflect the combined value, not just the sum of individual posts. Our calculator's deliverables basket helps you model this automatically.

## Follow Up (Once)

If you don't hear back in 5–7 business days, send one polite follow-up. Reference your original email and restate your rate range briefly. More than one follow-up risks damaging the relationship.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Pitching without knowing your rate
- Leading with follower count instead of views
- Sending generic copy-paste emails with no brand-specific detail
- Accepting the first offer without negotiating
- Forgetting to disclose sponsored content per [FTC endorsement guidelines](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/disclosures-101-social-media-influencers)

## Negotiation Tips

When a brand counters below your range, ask which deliverables they need and adjust the package rather than slashing your per-post rate. Removing Stories or shortening bio link duration preserves your value while meeting budget constraints.

Start with the numbers, write a tight pitch, and iterate based on responses. Every "no" refines your approach for the next brand.
