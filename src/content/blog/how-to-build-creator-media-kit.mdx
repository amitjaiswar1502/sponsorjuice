A media kit (or rate deck) is a creator's professional resume. When pitching brand partners or responding to inbound sponsorship inquiries, sending a clean, data-driven media kit sets you apart from amateur accounts and justifies premium pricing.

In this guide, we break down the 6 essential sections every creator media kit must include, along with tips on showcasing rates and audience demographics.

> **TL;DR**: Keep your media kit to 1 to 2 pages (PDF or digital link). Highlight average views, audience location split, past brand case studies, and package pricing options.

---

## 1. The 6 Core Elements of a High-Converting Media Kit

### 1. Bio & Channel Value Proposition
Keep your intro to 2–3 sentences. Define your content niche, main platform handle, and the core value you provide to your community (e.g., "Helping 45,000 developers master full-stack web development and productivity tools").

### 2. Verified Reach & View Metrics
Don't rely solely on total follower count. Brands prioritize reach and engagement:
- **Average Views per Post:** (Crucial metric for YouTube, TikTok, and Reels)
- **Total Follower / Subscriber Count**
- **Average Engagement Rate:** `(Likes + Comments + Shares) / Followers × 100`
- **Monthly Impressions / Reach:** Screenshots from native platform analytics.

### 3. Audience Demographics
Brand managers need to verify that your audience aligns with their target customer profile:
- **Top Geographic Locations:** (e.g., 62% United States, 14% Canada, 10% UK)
- **Age Breakdown:** (e.g., 65% 18–34 years old)
- **Gender Split:** (e.g., 55% Male / 45% Female)

---

## 2. Past Case Studies & Brand Logos

If you've previously partnered with brands, include a dedicated "Past Sponsorships" section. Showing logo recognition builds instant credibility.

- **Include Mini Case Studies:** Instead of just listing brand logos, share tangible performance stats:
  - *Example:* "Partnered with TechBrand to produce 1 TikTok video resulting in 42,000 views, 1,200 landing page clicks, and a 4.2% click-through rate."

---

## 3. How to Present Your Sponsorship Rates

Should you list exact rates in your media kit or keep rates custom?

### Strategy A: Starting Rate Card (Recommended for Speed)
Listing baseline starting rates (e.g., *"Packages starting at $500"*) filters out low-budget buyers while providing a starting anchor for negotiation.

### Strategy B: Custom Quotes Only
For large channels or complex multi-platform campaigns, list your available deliverables (e.g., Dedicated Video, Integrated Segment, Story Package, Newsletter Placement) and state *"Custom packages available upon request."*

## 4. Design & Format Recommendations

1. **Format as PDF or Web Link:** Always save your media kit as a high-resolution PDF or hosted web page. Never send raw Word documents or unformatted image files.
2. **Clean Typography & Brand Colors:** Use 2 primary brand colors and clear fonts. Avoid cluttered layouts or hard-to-read background images.
3. **Hyperlink Your Accounts:** Make sure your social handle links are clickable directly within the PDF document so brand managers can verify your profile with one click.
4. **Update Quarterly:** Set a recurring calendar reminder every 90 days to refresh view counts, follower numbers, and audience location graphs.
