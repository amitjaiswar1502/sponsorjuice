Instagram Reels sponsorship rates in 2026 typically run higher than static feed posts because Reels earn more organic reach through the Reels tab and Explore distribution. Creators who price Reels the same as a single feed photo often undercharge for the additional exposure brands receive.

> **TL;DR**: Instagram creators charge $0.007 to $0.015 per average view for sponsored Reels and feed content, with Reels often matching or exceeding static post rates. Bundle Reels, Stories, and bio links into packages for better overall compensation.

## What is an Instagram Reels sponsorship rate?

An Instagram Reels sponsorship rate is the fee a creator charges for a brand-sponsored Reel — a short-form vertical video posted to Instagram. Reels can also be bundled with Stories, feed posts, and bio link placements in a single brand deal package.

Reels rates are not standardized by Instagram; they are negotiated between creators and brands based on reach, niche, audience quality, and deliverable scope.

## Reels vs feed post rates — how do they compare?

| Deliverable | Typical rate weight | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Dedicated Reel | 100% (base rate) | Maximum reach, product demos |
| Feed post (static) | 80–100% | Polished brand imagery, carousels |
| Stories (3 frames) | 40% | Limited-time offers, swipe-ups |
| Bio link (7 days) | 30% | Conversion campaigns |

Reels often match or slightly exceed static feed post rates because of higher average reach. If your Reels consistently outperform feed posts, price them at or above your feed rate — not below.

## How to calculate your Instagram Reels rate

**Rate = average Reel views × $0.007–$0.015 × niche multiplier × geography weight**

Geography weights used by SponsorJuice:

- US/Canada: 1.5×
- UK/Australia: 1.3×
- Global: 0.7×

### Worked example

A beauty creator averaging 15,000 Reel views with a US/Canada audience:

- Low: 15,000 × $0.007 × 1.2 × 1.5 = **$189**
- High: 15,000 × $0.015 × 1.2 × 1.5 = **$405**

Add Stories and a 7-day bio link to build a package worth $250–$550.

## Building an Instagram sponsorship package

Don't sell a single Reel in isolation. Brands prefer bundled deliverables:

1. **1 dedicated Reel** — primary content piece
2. **3 Story frames** — behind-the-scenes or CTA slides
3. **1 feed carousel** — repurposed Reel stills or product photos
4. **7-day bio link** — drives traffic to a landing page

## What brands look for in Instagram creators

Brands evaluating Instagram partnerships typically review:

- **Average Reel views** over the last 15–20 posts (excluding viral outliers)
- **Audience geography** — US/CA audiences command premium CPMs
- **Niche alignment** — finance and tech convert better for B2B brands
- **Engagement quality** — saves and shares signal purchase intent more than likes

Reference the [FTC Disclosures Guide](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/disclosures-101-social-media-influencers) when posting sponsored Reels — clear disclosure is required for paid partnerships.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I charge for an Instagram Reel in 2026?

Most creators charge $0.007 to $0.015 per average view. A creator with 10,000 Reel views in the tech niche with a US audience might charge $105–$225 per dedicated Reel.

### Are Instagram Reels worth more than feed posts?

Often yes. Reels typically earn higher reach through algorithmic distribution. If your Reels average more views than feed posts, price them at or above your feed rate.

### Should I include Stories in an Instagram brand deal?

Yes. Stories add value with limited-time CTAs and swipe-up links. Three Story frames typically add 40% weight to a package price in SponsorJuice's deliverables model.

### How do I pitch an Instagram sponsorship rate?

Lead with average Reel views, audience location, and niche. Provide a rate range, not a fixed price. Link your media kit and personalize the email with a reference to the brand's product or recent campaign.
